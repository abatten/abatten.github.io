My Research

My research is to looking at the metallicity of early galaxies and the intergalactic medium during the Epoch of Reionisation. I am taking a two pronged approach to my research.

First is using The Aurora Simulations performed by Pawlik et al. 2016

The first galaxies formed in the initial billion years after the Big Bang, rapidly forming stars which form heavier elements, termed metals, through fusion of hydrogen and helium. As those stars explode they eject these newly formed metals into the galaxy and ultimately into the intergalactic medium between galaxies. These metals can be seen by chance alignments of bright background objects, in particular quasars (a galaxy with an extremely luminous nucleus), shining through the material, revealed as an absorption spectrum. Determining the manner in which these metals pollute the intergalactic medium, potentially to be re-accreted into the growing galaxies at later times, is a fundamental question in astronomy. Piecing the fragmentary sightlines taken with the largest telescopes into the bigger picture requires cutting-edge galaxy simulations.

At Swinburne University and through national allocation by Australia Astronomy Limited we have access to observational resources such as Keck and VLT to explore the early, and therefore distant, Universe. Associate Professor Emma Ryan-Weber is a world-renowned expert in these observations and will be able to aid the candidate in learning to reduce these datasets. Principal Supervisor, Associate Professor Alan Duffy, is an expert in cosmological simulations and will guide the candidate in exploring simulated universes such as Aurora.

One aspect of this project will involve using an existing MUSE/VLT cube of data along the line of sight to a redshift 6 quasar. The MUSE cube reveals low luminosity galaxies close the metal absorption line systems. The existing automatic galaxy finding code written for MUSE does not detect all of these galaxies. We would like to test a different code (SOFIA), initially written for radio data cube, but no one has ever tried it on an optical data cube.
